The Sound and Culture

Deep house is an integral part of electronic dance music's (EDM) rich tapestry, with its roots tracing back to the early 1990s. This sonic landscape is characterized by a slower tempo range, typically between 118-130 BPM, which lends itself perfectly to crafting intricate melodies and harmonies that ebb and flow like the tides. The genre's evolution has been shaped by pioneers like Larry Heard (Mr. Fingers), Frankie Knuckles, and Ron Trent, who expertly blended soulful elements with the raw energy of house music.
