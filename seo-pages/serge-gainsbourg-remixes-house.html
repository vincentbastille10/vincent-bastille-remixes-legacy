Gainsbourg's Influence on Early French Touch

Serge Gainsbourg's pioneering work on Les Rythmes à Travers les Ondes, a 1963 album that blended pop, rock, and jazz elements, laid the groundwork for the French Touch movement. This genre, which would come to define the sound of French house in the 1990s, was characterized by its use of lush, atmospheric soundscapes and its fusion of organic and electronic elements. Gainsbourg's innovative approach to music, which combined elements of pop, rock, and electronic music, had a profound impact on the development of French Touch, influencing artists such as Air, Phoenix, and Daft Punk, among others.
