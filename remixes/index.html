The Artist

DJ since 1995

It all started in Angers in 1995 — a time when house music was still a revelation, a secret language that very few people spoke fluently. Vincent Bastille learned it from the ground up: the kick drum at 124 BPM, filtered synths, gospel vocals that transform a dance floor into something almost sacred.

A residency in Athens. Five years in the Paris Marais. Sets in Ibiza and New York. A career built on the conviction that a DJ is not a jukebox — they're a narrator. Every mix is a story. Every transition is a decision.

On the production side, the remixes have left a clear imprint: Jennifer Lopez, Madonna, Sade, Modjo, Michael Jackson. The Madonna remix for the Beatport World Challenge finished 2nd globally. The Jennifer Lopez remix generated millions of streams. That's not luck — it's an ear trained on thirty years of dancefloors.

Today, the Bandcamp catalogue holds the essentials: originals, remixes, remastered archives, instrumentals. 25+ releases spanning thirty years of house music in all its forms.

Archive

Legacy &
Archives

Before the Madonna remixes and the Beatport rankings, there were the first tracks — recorded without a safety net, at a time when French house music was still searching for its voice. These archives have been restored, remastered, and published on Bandcamp.

The Legacy album gathers remastered originals from the 2000s: Percubata, Africaans, Princess Liquid — tracks that define the aesthetic of the era. The Gumbing album reaches even further back into the West-African groove that has always run through the work.

The Remixes (Legacy) compilation chronicles twenty years of reinterpretations: versions that never had an official release, DJ edits, bootlegs that became set classics.
