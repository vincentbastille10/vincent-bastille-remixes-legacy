The Birth of French Touch

French Touch, a genre born from the union of French electronic music and the nostalgic sounds of the 1970s and 1980s, is a genre that has left an indelible mark on the music world. The likes of Modjo and Lady, with their signature blend of funk, disco, and house, set the tone for the era. Their influence can be seen in the works of later artists, including Daft Punk, Cassius, and Stardust, who have all paid homage to the original sound. Vincent Bastille, with his latest album, 'Vincent Bastille Remixes Legacy', is the latest to join the ranks, offering a fresh take on the classic sound.

The Evolution of French Touch

French Touch, much like any genre, is not immune to change. As the years went by, the sound evolved, incorporating new elements and influences. Artists like Bob Sinclar and his 'Sauvage' album, which blended French Touch with Latin rhythms, pushed the genre further. Today, artists like Vincent Bastille continue to evolve the sound, drawing inspiration from a wide range of sources. His remixes of Jennifer Lopez, Madonna, and many more, a testament to his skill and creativity, showcase his ability to adapt and evolve the genre.
